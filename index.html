What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems.

It encompasses learning, reasoning, and self-correction. From the algorithms that recommend your next favorite song to the autonomous vehicles navigating our streets, AI is the invisible force reshaping our reality.

A Brief History

1950

The Turing Test

Alan Turing proposes the "Imitation Game" to test machine intelligence.

1956

Birth of AI

The term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined at the Dartmouth Conference.

1997

Deep Blue

IBM's Deep Blue beats world chess champion Garry Kasparov.

2012

Deep Learning Boom

AlexNet dominates the ImageNet competition, sparking the modern AI revolution.

2022+

Generative Era

Models like GPT-4 and Midjourney redefine creativity and conversation.

Types of AI

🎯

ANI

Artificial Narrow Intelligence

AI designed for a specific task (e.g., Siri, Chess bots, facial recognition). The only AI that exists today.

🧠

AGI

Artificial General Intelligence

A theoretical AI with the ability to understand, learn, and apply knowledge across a wide variety of tasks, matching human intellect.

✨

ASI

Artificial Super Intelligence

An intellect that is much smarter than the best human brains in practically every field, including scientific creativity, general wisdom, and social skills.
